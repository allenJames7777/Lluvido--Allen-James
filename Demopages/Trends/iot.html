Smart Watch

A smartwatch is a portable device that's designed to be worn on a wrist. Like smartphones, they use touchscreens, offer apps,
and often record your heart rate and other vital signs.Steve Mann, an inventor from Canada, arguably designed
the first smartwatch in 1998.

Cyclometer

Bike computers attach to your handlebars or stem and display a range of real-time information on your speed, distance, trip time and much more.
They have soared in popularity in conjunction with the growth in performance software, such as Strava,
providing a world of statistics and data for cyclists everywhere. German Inventor Karl von Drais is credited with developing the first bicycle.
His machine, known as the "swiftwalker," hit the road in 1817.

Smart Tv

Smart TVs. Also known as an Internet TV, a Smart TV is any TV that can provide additional programs through internet connectivity.
It's like having a computer built into your TV. Though there had been earlier innovations, the first ever patent for a Smart TV was granted in 1994
to a French firm (“Fast France Advanced Systems”). But it was not until 2007 when the first Smart TV, HP's Mediasmart TV (SL3760), was actually developed.

Fitness Band

A mobile device worn on the wrist, often with a display that can be used to control a smartphone or to keep track of information such as fitness levels.
Fitness trackers, as we know them today, first surfaced in 1965 with the Manpo-kei, which translates to '10,000 steps meter' and was invented by Dr Yoshiro Hatano.
